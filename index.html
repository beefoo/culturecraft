CultureCraft

Paint with open culture

Try the app
A screen recording of CultureCraft app that has a pointer brushing cut-out pieces of visual art onto a canvas

CultureCraft is like a simple painting program, but instead of painting with colors, you are painting with fragments of visual culture from renowned museums and libraries such as the Met, the Smithsonian, and the New York Public Library.

Featured collections

  • Open Access at The Met

    The Metropolitan Museum of Art is the most popular art museum in the United States.

  • Smithsonian Open Access

    The Smithsonian Institution is the world's largest museum, education, and research complex.

  • NYPL Public Domain Collections

  • Biodiversity Heritage Library

    The Biodiversity Heritage Library is the world's largest open access digital library for biodiversity literature and archives.

  • NASA Image and Video Library

    The National Aeronautics and Space Administration is the global leader in space exploration.

  • Citizen DJ at the Library of Congress

About this project

CultureCraft was created by Brian Foo as a way to amplify discovery and reuse of open access visual material from museums and libraries from across the country. The tool itself is intentionally minimal with a very low barrier to entry. Its lack of features and controls encourages the user to focus on play and serendipity rather than trying to make something in particular.

The tool itself is open source. If you are an organization with an open access visual collection that you would like to use in a tool like this, feel free to contact me!
